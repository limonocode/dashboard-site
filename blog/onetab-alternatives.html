OneTab alternatives that don't lose your tabs

Updated July 2026

OneTab has millions of users and one job: collapse your tabs into a list. It does that fast. But read recent reviews and the same complaint repeats across years: saved tabs disappearing after updates, groups flattened into a plain list, uninstalling deletes everything, and no version history to recover from. If your tab list matters, "fast but occasionally gone" is a bad trade.

What to demand from a replacement

  • Survivability — data stored properly (not in a fragile format), export at any time.
  • Structure — real groups with names, not one endless list.
  • Findability — search; a list you can't search is a black hole with extra steps.
  • Honest pricing — several popular managers moved once-free features behind subscriptions.

Why we built something wider than a tab manager

Saving tabs is one habit out of several. The same person also saves posts on Instagram and Reddit, articles "for later", and bookmarks — five black holes instead of one. Dashboard New Tab treats them as one problem: save a whole window as a named group in one click (like OneTab, but with structure), then import your saved posts from nine platforms next to them. Everything is stored locally, searchable with operators, and exportable to JSON/Markdown — so the OneTab failure mode (trust, then loss) is designed out.

Rule of thumb: if a tool can't show you an export button, don't feed it years of your attention.
