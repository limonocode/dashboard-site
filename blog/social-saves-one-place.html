Where do your Instagram, TikTok and Reddit saves actually go?

Updated July 2026

Everyone does it: tap save on an Instagram reel, a TikTok, a Reddit thread, a YouTube video — with the honest intention of coming back. Research on saving behavior is brutal about what happens next: in one study only 16% of saved items were ever retrieved. The platforms make it worse on purpose or by neglect:

  • Reddit: one endless "saved" list. No search, no folders.
  • Instagram: collections exist, but you can't search inside them or get anything out.
  • TikTok: favorites buried four taps deep.
  • YouTube: Watch Later with 500 videos is not a plan, it's a museum. The official Watch Later API was closed back in 2016.
  • Telegram: "Saved Messages" quietly becomes your main bookmark manager — with zero tools around it.

Why no app has fixed this

There is no official API for saved posts on Instagram, TikTok or Facebook, and Reddit closed API registration in 2026. Tools that scrape your feed with your password risk getting your account banned — which is why we checked twenty competitors and found none that cover all major platforms.

The legal loophole platforms gave you themselves

Privacy law (GDPR and friends) obliges every platform to hand you your data. Buried in every settings page is a “Download your information” button that produces a file — and that file contains your saves. That's the whole trick:

  1. Request the export (Instagram: Settings → Your information → Download; TikTok: Settings → Account → Download your data → JSON; Reddit: reddit.com/settings/data-request; Telegram Desktop: Settings → Advanced → Export).
  2. Drop the file into Dashboard New Tab — the parsing happens locally in your browser. No passwords, no scraping, no ban risk, nothing uploaded.
  3. Your saves from nine platforms land as groups on your new tab: searchable, taggable, with duplicate cleanup and read-later queues.

Getting them back out of the black hole

Collecting is half the job. The other half is seeing saves again: Dashboard resurfaces a “forgotten gem” on the new tab you open dozens of times a day, shows what you saved “a year ago today”, and gives you a triage mode to sweep an inbox of new saves in minutes. And everything exports to JSON or Markdown whenever you want to leave.

Your saves are a diary of what caught your mind. They deserve better than four separate black holes.
